Definitions. Whole counting: a co-authored paper credits every author fully — it rewards collaboration and matches how institutions count. Fractional: each paper contributes 1/(number of authors) — a per-head share that cannot be inflated by joining large teams. Citations are whole-counted sums over the researcher's TRUBA papers (snapshot at extraction; recent papers are citation-censored). Q1 share is among the researcher's SJR-ranked papers. Only authors with a Turkish affiliation on the paper are counted; aggregation is by Scopus Author ID (byline spelling variants merge). Names link to public Scopus author profiles.
