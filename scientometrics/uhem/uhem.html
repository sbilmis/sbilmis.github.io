Where it began
2008
First UHeM acknowledgment in Scopus

An experimental and quantum mechanical study on electrochemical properties of N-substituted pyrroles — Tuzun N. et al., Journal of Molecular Structure THEOCHEM, 2008-05-30. DOI

The earliest paper to credit UHeM in its funding text — a computational study still cited 13 times. It opens 2008–2026, 674 acknowledged articles of national high-performance computing.

Methodology & audit
42%
UHeM's own publications page lists 229 articles (2008–2018); matched by DOI, 87 of 205 also appear in this acknowledgment corpus. We then looked up every non-matching DOI in Scopus: 106 are indexed but have no funding/acknowledgment text extracted by Scopus at all, 11 are not in Scopus, and none credit a different centre. So the shortfall is Scopus's incomplete funding-text coverage — not authors failing to acknowledge UHeM. Acknowledgment mining is therefore a lower bound set by database coverage (thinnest for older papers — their list ends 2018, while this corpus runs to 2026).
How UHeM papers are identified

Corpus definition. Scopus indexes the funding/acknowledgment text of papers; the FUND-ALL field searches it. UHeM papers are the union of the acknowledgment variants validated against live Scopus counts:

  • FUND-ALL("UHeM") — the acronym (613)
  • OR FUND-ALL("UYBHM") — the former name (+79)
  • OR FUND-ALL("National Center for High Performance Computing of Turkey") — precise long form (+13)

The bare phrase "National Center for High Performance Computing" is never used alone: it collides with Taiwan's NCHC (5,367 hits). A review queue of loosely-phrased candidates was classified by reading each paper's funding text — 22 genuine UHeM papers were recovered and folded in; Taiwan/India false positives were excluded. Turkish-language phrasings add nothing (Scopus extracts funding text in English).

Document-type policy. Journal articles drive every quality metric (quartiles, open access, citations-per-article, leaderboards). The 61 conference papers are reported separately.

Quartiles from SCImago (SJR) tables, journal's publication-year quartile, joined by Scopus source-id then ISSN. Open access uses Scopus's own OA flag. Researchers are aggregated by Scopus Author ID (not name) and restricted to Turkish affiliations.

Raw Scopus records are not redistributed (Elsevier terms). UHeM is İTÜ's national HPC centre — distinct from TRUBA (TÜBİTAK ULAKBİM); this dashboard is the UHeM counterpart of the TRUBA one.
