WLCG / Collaboration Segment

◂ back to the TRUBA dashboard · large-collaboration papers whose Turkish computing ran at ULAKBİM · snapshot 2026-07-13
This segment is reported separately from the main dashboard on purpose. These papers do not acknowledge TRUBA in the usual way and carry a very different citation profile (118,803 citations across 2,098 articles), so mixing them into the acknowledgment corpus would distort every average. Attribution differs per experiment: ATLAS and CMS (WLCG Tier-2 hosted at ULAKBİM) credit the Worldwide LHC Computing Grid generically — their series here are collaboration articles with Turkish co-authors, and ULAKBİM's role is documented in the ATLAS Computing Acknowledgements. Belle II (ULAKBİM raw-data centre) credits “TUBITAK ULAKBIM (Turkey)” directly in its funding text and has no Turkish co-authors, so its series is the large-collaboration papers carrying that credit (sample Crossref-verified).

Where this segment began · 2008
The earliest paper in the segment is CMS's “The CMS experiment at the CERN LHC”, Journal of Instrumentation (2008-01) — the experiment's foundational detector paper. Note the timing: TRUBA's own first acknowledgment (2006) predates the collaboration era; ULAKBİM's original Beowulf/T3E supercomputers came first, the grid role followed.
